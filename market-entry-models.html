Partnership models available

Technology License
Factory pays a license fee per kg produced. Fresh Asia retains full IP. Factory sells under Fresh Asia brand. Most IP-protective arrangement.
Contract Manufacturing
Factory produces to Fresh Asia's specification. Fresh Asia (or local partner) handles sales and distribution. Clean separation of manufacturing and brand.
Joint Venture
Shared entity between Fresh Asia and factory partner. Aligns incentives most strongly. Higher legal complexity but shared risk and investment.

What the factory partner brings

  • Existing HACCP, IFS, and/or BRC certifications
  • Facility space (200–500m² for initial line)
  • Trained labour force already in food production
  • Cold chain infrastructure (at SVOBODA)
  • Existing retail supply relationships (Albert, Kaufland, Lidl, Tesco)
  • Local market knowledge and supplier networks

Government incentives available

If Fresh Asia's equipment investment exceeds ~€500K and creates local jobs, the Czech government's CzechInvest agency offers: corporate tax relief for up to 10 years; job creation grants of up to €12,000 per new position; training grants covering up to 70% of staff training costs; and cash grants on capital expenditure of up to 20% (South Moravia region). Investment incentives must be applied for before expenditure begins.

Identified factory targets

  • RACIO s.r.o. — Břeclav (50km). Rice-based, ISO/IFS/BRC certified. Best for rice noodles.
  • JAPAVO spol. s r.o. — Slušovice (55km). Pasta manufacturer, private-label mindset. Best for wheat noodles.
  • SVOBODA knedlíků — Blučina (20km). Chilled dough specialist, all major retail. Best for bao buns.
  • VIMIXA a.s. — Telnice (15km). Existing Asian noodle producer — upgrade pitch.
Advantages
  • Fastest path to production (9–12 months)
  • Zero certification wait — already in place
  • Retail access via factory's existing supply agreements
  • Lowest capital requirement for Fresh Asia
  • No real estate or construction risk
  • Scalable — add lines or factories without asset commitment
  • Fresh Asia retains full IP ownership throughout
Considerations
  • Production scheduling shared with factory's other lines
  • Margin shared with factory partner
  • Quality control relies partly on partner's systems
  • Technology IP is contractually protected but known to factory
  • Dependency on partner's continued cooperation

Investment by scale

  • Small (500kg/day): €460K–€760K total Year 1 — facility fit-out €80–150K, production line €150–250K, certifications €30–60K, staff €120–180K/yr
  • Medium (2t/day): €1.0M–€1.6M — production line €300–600K, fit-out €200–400K
  • Large (5t+/day): €2.5M–€4.5M — full multi-line facility

Czech cost advantages

  • Industrial space: €4–7/m²/month vs. €10–18/m² in Germany
  • Manufacturing labour: ~€1,860/month avg vs. €3,400 in Germany
  • Corporate tax: 21% vs. 25–30% in Germany, Netherlands
  • CzechInvest grants: up to 20% cash grant on capital investment in South Moravian region
  • Tax relief: up to 10 years corporate income tax reduction

Recommended approach: Lease space in an existing industrial park near Brno (Slatina, Modřice, Šlapanice) rather than building from scratch. This reduces the construction timeline to 12–18 months instead of 24–42, and avoids land acquisition complexity. Industrial park rental in South Moravia is €4–7/m²/month — among the most competitive in Central Europe.

Advantages
  • Full operational control — production schedule, quality, priorities entirely Fresh Asia's
  • Maximum margin — no factory partner taking a cut
  • Builds a standalone asset (real balance sheet value)
  • Strongest CzechInvest incentive eligibility
  • Maximum IP protection — technology stays within Fresh Asia's own walls
  • Scalable without needing partner agreement
Considerations
  • 12–18 months to obtain retail certifications (IFS/BRC)
  • Full operational burden from day one — HR, logistics, compliance, QA
  • High fixed cost base before revenue is established
  • 18–42 months before first production
  • Most appropriate after market demand is proven via Model A

Option 3 — Accelerated ownership path

Acquire an Existing Factory

Buying a certified, operating food manufacturer gives the benefits of ownership — certifications, workforce, retail relationships — without the years-long wait to build them. The right acquisition compresses the timeline to full control significantly.

What to look for in an acquisition target

  • IFS Food or BRC certified (required for Czech retail)
  • 100–1,000 tonnes/year existing capacity
  • Dough-based, pasta, or rice-product manufacturing background
  • Within 150km of Brno
  • Existing cold chain infrastructure
  • Existing retail supply agreements (Albert, Kaufland, Lidl, etc.)
  • Owner approaching retirement or business in transition

How to find acquisition targets

  • M&A advisors specialising in CEE food sector (KPMG, Deloitte local offices, boutique Czech M&A firms)
  • Czech business registry (ares.cz) — search for food manufacturers in South Moravia
  • Direct approach to identified factory partners (RACIO, JAPAVO, SVOBODA) — even if not publicly for sale, ownership discussions can be initiated
  • CzechInvest brownfield database — lists former food facilities

Strategic note: The most elegant acquisition path may be to start with Model A — a factory partnership with RACIO or SVOBODA — and, once the production line is embedded and generating revenue, approach the factory owner about acquisition. At that point, both parties know each other well, the production line is already installed, and the price negotiation is grounded in real performance data rather than projections.

Advantages
  • Certifications inherited — skips 12–18 month wait
  • Workforce and operational knowledge acquired
  • Retail relationships may transfer with the business
  • Full control from acquisition date
  • Creates a real asset — standalone business with balance sheet value
  • Faster than greenfield if right target found
Considerations
  • Requires full due diligence — legal, financial, environmental
  • Hidden liabilities possible (debt, audit issues, lease terms)
  • Right target must be found and available — takes 6–18 months
  • Integration complexity — blending two businesses takes time
  • Highest capital requirement

Decision framework

Side-by-Side Comparison

The three models serve different stages of market maturity. The recommended path is sequential — start with the partnership model to prove demand, then escalate to ownership once the market is established.

Recommended sequence: Begin with Model A to establish market presence with minimum capital and maximum speed. Use the 2–3 years of partnership operation to build HoReCa clients, enter retail through the factory's existing supply agreements, and generate real Czech market revenue data. If volumes and demand justify it, pursue Model C (acquire the factory partner or an equivalent) or Model B (lease and build a dedicated facility). This approach minimises risk at each stage while preserving optionality at every step.
