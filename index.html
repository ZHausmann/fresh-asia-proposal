The opportunity

A market with real potential

Asian food is the fastest-growing ethnic food segment in Europe. The Czech market has established producers of pre-cooked, shelf-stable wheat noodles — but fresh/chilled rice noodles, rice sheets, and chilled bao buns remain an open category with no identified commercial-scale local producer.

Competitive landscape

What already exists locally

Local Production — Czech Republic has a strong network of certified food manufacturers producing noodles, rice-based products, and dough-based foods — many already supplying CEE markets.
Production is mainly focused on dry and shelf-stable products, with no large-scale manufacturers of fresh or chilled Asian formats in the CZ or neighbouring countries.
Sapa Market (Prague) — Small-scale daily fresh rice noodles for the local Vietnamese community. No retail or HoReCa-scale distribution.

Where Fresh Asia is differentiated

Fresh/chilled rice noodles & rice sheets — no identified Czech manufacturer at commercial scale.
Chilled bao buns — existing options are frozen only. No chilled, preservative-free bao bun at retail or HoReCa scale in the Czech market.
75-day shelf life, preservative-free — Fresh Asia's patented packaging process is not replicated by any identified local competitor.
Western-palate adaptation — products developed specifically for European taste and portioning, not Asian formats imported as-is.

Factory partner identification

Certified manufacturers — identified and ready to approach

We have identified a shortlist of certified food manufacturers in the Czech Republic with strong potential for partnership. These facilities specialise in pasta, noodle, rice-based, and chilled dough products — categories that are technically compatible with Fresh Asia's production model. All identified factories operate at industrial scale and supply major retail chains and HoReCa clients across Central Europe.

What these factories already have

  • —Established production infrastructure for dough, noodle, or rice-based processing
  • —Experience in high-volume manufacturing and private-label production
  • —Existing distribution to HoReCa and retail
  • —Cold-chain capabilities in selected facilities for chilled product categories

How partners are selected

  • —Technical compatibility — rice processing, noodle production, or dough and steaming capabilities
  • —Ability to integrate new production lines without major structural changes
  • —Certification level and audit readiness
  • —Existing relationships with retail and HoReCa clients
  • —Operational flexibility and openness to licensing or contract manufacturing

Certifications & standards

The Czech food manufacturing sector operates under strict EU regulations, with most producers holding internationally recognised certifications:

BRCGS

Strategic advantage

The key opportunity lies not in building new production from scratch, but in adapting existing certified facilities to produce fresh Asian formats.

This approach significantly reduces time to market, capital investment, and operational risk — while enabling rapid entry into both Czech and wider CEE markets.
